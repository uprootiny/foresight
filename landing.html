The Conceptual Core

Causal discovery is geometry of information. Every method differs only by what Hilbert space it embeds into, how it approximates projections, and how it tests orthogonality. Here is the 10-step machine:

0
The Concrete Problem
Observe X_t, Y_t. Does Y → X?
Two signals. Does knowing Y's past improve prediction of X's future?
1
Build Predictor Spaces
H_X = span{X_{t-1},...,X_{t-L}} vs H_{X,Y}
Restricted (X only) vs unrestricted (X+Y) lag spaces.
2
Compute Projections (OLS)
X̂ = P_H(X_{t+1}) — orthogonal projection
Best linear prediction = projection onto the past subspace. Show β coefficients, R².
3
Compute Innovations (Residuals)
ε_{t+1} = X_{t+1} − P_H(X_{t+1})
The unpredictable part. Orthogonal to the past. The irreducible uncertainty.
4
Test Orthogonality
⟨ε^(X), Y_t⟩ ≠ 0 ⇒ Y causes X
If the restricted residual correlates with Y, then Y carries information about X's future.
5
Residual Energy Reduction = Granger
‖ε^{X,Y}‖ < ‖ε^{X}‖ ⇒ causality
Classical Granger test. F-statistic. Equivalent to comparing projection distances in L².
6
Why Linear Projections Fail
X = sin(Y) + Y² + ε
Nonlinear relationships are invisible to linear projections. We need a bigger Hilbert space.
7
Lift into RKHS — Kernel Granger
φ(x) ∈ H_k, k(x,y) = exp(−‖x−y‖²/2σ²)
RBF kernel lifts data into infinite-dimensional space where nonlinear becomes linear.
8
HSIC — Cross-Covariance Operator
‖C_{XY}‖²_HS = 0 ⇔ X ⊥ Y
Hilbert-Schmidt Independence Criterion. Measures ALL dependence (linear + nonlinear) via operator norm.
9
Time Direction Emerges
H_{t-2} ⊂ H_{t-1} ⊂ H_t
Asymmetry in residual reduction IS the arrow of causation. No direction imposed — it emerges from nested past subspaces.
10
Verdict
Which variables change the orthogonal decomposition?
Compare forward vs reverse, linear vs kernel. The geometry tells the story.
